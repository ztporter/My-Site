Student Researcher

University of Kentucky | Lexington,Ky | Feb 2015 - present

Supervisor: Dr. James Lumpp | jel@uky.edu

I worked in the Space Systems Lab as a student researcher funded by a NASA EPSCoR grant to rebuild a previous CubeSat, Ky-Sat 2, for a launch off of the International Space Station. I collaborated with other students to redesign the electric power system used on the satellite which included the solar panels, battery modules, battery protection circuitry, and telemetry monitoring. I used Altium to design the printed circuit boards for the EPS as well as the solar module boards, and to capture the circuit schematics. We also disassembled the previous satellite to test the radio and other parts for functionality, and redesigned the inhibit system of the satellite to comply with new regulations.

Early Revision PCB for the EPS Battery Module

Undergraduate Researcher

University of Kentucky | Lexington,Ky | Mar 2013 - Aug 2014

Supervisor: Dr. James Lumpp | jel@uky.edu

As an undergraduate student in Electrical Engineering I worked on various aerospace projects in the Space Systems Lab as an undergraduate student researcher. I developed a payload for a high altitude balloon launch that utilized a GPS and servo motor controlled by an Arduino to deploy a tray of aerogel for micrometeorite capture and then reseal the tray before descending. The full details of the project are available here. I also learned how to use Altium Designer to capture circuit schematics and design PCB's for various circuits including telemetry chips, embedded circuits, and an arm tracking device. Most of the projects in the lab focused on embedded systems so I learned how to program ARM microcontrollers using the Keil uVision IDE, Arduinos, and other scripts in Python or Matlab for data processing.
