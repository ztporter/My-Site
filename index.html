I am an electrical engineer with a focus on designing embedded systems in aerospace applications who has slowly been pulled to the dark side of software engineering. I try to straddle the formiddable chasm between hardware and software which usually makes me either the most hardware-oriented of the software engineers or the most software-minded of the hardware designers. If I go too many days without getting to probe a circuit with my logic analyzer or oscilloscope I start to get a little antsy at my terminal and if I spend too long without getting to code anything my eyes start to glaze over and schematic symbols all start to blur together.

I also have a masters degree in Philosophy and have taught several courses in deductive logic, critical thinking, and scientific reasoning. I typically lie about having a philosophy degree so that strange people don't ask me obscure questions and normal people don't think that there's something deeply wrong with me.
